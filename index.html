About Me

My computer science journey began at UC Santa Cruz in Fall 2013. I had entered college with little to no knowledge of what I wanted to pursue. I decided to take my first programming class; in this class I learned how to code in java, and I quickly fell in love with the world of technology.

I first became interested in Data Science when I was accepted for a undergraduate research position in Data Analysis and Visualization my Freshman year. I had gotten my first experience with R Studio, and little did I know that Data Science would play a large part in my life the following years.

My Sohpomore year, I took a Web Applications class. This class gave me my first taste of web development. Although I had experience with html/css, I was able to gain extensive experience in not only html/css, but python and javascript as well.

When I transferred to UC Berkeley in Fall 2015, I was able to combine my two interests. I joined the Berkeley Institute for Data Science, and in the fall, and in the spring I became the head of the Infrastructure Team. The Infrastructure Team's main focus was to develop websites pertaining to the Data Science program/curriculum at UC Berkeley.

Last summer during my second internship at Verizon, I was able to work together in a group setting to develop a cross-platform mobile application. This gave me my first taste of mobile development. I look forward to creating more mobile applications in the future.
